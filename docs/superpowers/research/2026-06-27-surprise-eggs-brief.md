# Research Brief — 🥚 Surprise Eggs

**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Mode:** A new game mode for *Zoe and Desi's Rainbow Unicorn Adventures* (Phaser 4 + TypeScript + Vite).
**Concept (from grilled intake):** An **active-hatching** sandbox. **4 big eggs wobble in place** on a barnyard scene; the child hatches each with **3 taps** — every tap lands a visible crack stage (*crack… crack… BURST*) — then the shell shatters and a **random cute animal** pops out with a fanfare. **Pure sandbox**: no score, no counter, no difficulty, no levels; each hatch is self-contained and always rewarding. After a hatch the animal lingers ~2–3s, fades, and a **fresh egg refills that spot** — endless. A rare **golden egg** spawns visibly shimmering (its contents decided at spawn) and hatches the **unicorn** jackpot with an extra-big party. Multi-touch (two kids at once), forgiving large hit area, reduced-motion + photosensitivity-safe.

**Identity vs. the existing modes:** the closest sibling is **Unicorn Gumballs** (tap → concealed random animal + fanfare), but that is *one passive machine* that does the work for you. Surprise Eggs puts the work in the child's fingers — **active, multi-target hatching with a child-driven progressive crack** — and is the only mode where a concealed reveal is something you *physically break open*. Distinct from Pop (destroys visible cuties, scored), Peekaboo (race to tap before they hide, scored), and Aquarium (tap drifting fish, never destroyed).

This brief summarizes three parallel Exa investigations (UX, Architecture, Tech Stack). It is authoritative input to the design spec.

---

## 1. UX — interaction patterns for a toddler hatch/reveal toy

### Key findings
- **The "concealed reveal" is compelling for 2–4s because of anticipation, not the prize.** Surprise-egg / unboxing appeal is a mini three-act arc — present the egg (exposition) → "what's inside / can I open it?" (rising tension) → reveal (resolution) — and the thrill lives in the build-up, validating a deliberate *crack…crack…BURST* over an instant pop. ([Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/positively-media/202002/why-do-kids-love-unboxing-videos), [Rutledge](https://www.pamelarutledge.com/why-do-kids-love-unboxing-videos/))
- **"Tap to cause an effect, repeat to re-trigger it" is the single most developmentally apt toddler interaction.** Cause-and-effect play is a documented milestone (combine an action to cause change ~18mo; predict outcomes ~36mo), and children repeat the action specifically to re-fire the effect — exactly the hatch loop. ([CA ITF cognitive foundations](https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/cd/re/itf09cogdevfdcae.asp))
- **Tap is the foundational child gesture; drag/double-tap/pinch are out under 4.** Kids think the app is "broken" when a double-tap is required because they expect a single touch to do something immediately. ([NN/g children physical dev](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/children-ux-physical-development/), [TIDRC PDF](https://stirlab.org/wp-content/uploads/A-Framework-of-Touchscreen-Interaction-Design-Recommendations-for-Children-TIDRC-Characterizing-the-Gap-between-Research-Evidence-and-Design-Practice.pdf))
- **Touch targets for young children should be ≥ 2cm × 2cm (4× the adult minimum), and the hit area should exceed the visible sprite.** TIDRC adds: accept tap times up to ~5s and target offsets of ~10mm, and register on touch-down — i.e. forgive sloppy, off-center, lingering taps. ([NN/g children](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/children-ux-physical-development/), [NN/g target size](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/touch-target-size/), [TIDRC PDF](https://stirlab.org/wp-content/uploads/A-Framework-of-Touchscreen-Interaction-Design-Recommendations-for-Children-TIDRC-Characterizing-the-Gap-between-Research-Evidence-and-Design-Practice.pdf))
- **Per-tap feedback must be concrete and on the object — avoid abstract progress bars under 4.** Each tap should land its own visible crack *on the egg* (not a meter), with simultaneous audio+visual within ~300–500ms. Children "tap too hard, long, or repeatedly until they see evidence it registered." ([Sesame Workshop PDF](https://joanganzcooneycenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/SesameWorkshop-2012.pdf), [TIDRC PDF](https://stirlab.org/wp-content/uploads/A-Framework-of-Touchscreen-Interaction-Design-Recommendations-for-Children-TIDRC-Characterizing-the-Gap-between-Research-Evidence-and-Design-Practice.pdf), [Rovio kids UX](https://medium.com/@roviobert/ui-ux-guidelines-for-ages-3-6-68f1c9656574))
- **The wobble "tell" primes anticipatory joy.** Peekaboo research shows toddler delight comes from surprise *balanced with expectation*; a predictable build-up (a wobble that signals "something's about to happen") produces the laughter and also reads as "I'm touchable." ([BBC Future](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20140417-why-all-babies-love-peekaboo), [Parrott & Gleitman](https://doi.org/10.1080/02699938908412710))
- **Multi-touch is safe *only* for "no correct answer" actions** — children trigger it unintentionally and with limited dexterity, so reserve it for non-correctness cases. Two kids tapping eggs in a sandbox is precisely the sanctioned case. ([Sesame Workshop PDF](https://joanganzcooneycenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/SesameWorkshop-2012.pdf))
- **A pure-delight sandbox is the explicit Toca Boca / Sago Mini model** — "digital toys, not games": no levels, instructions, win/lose, text/VO, IAP, or ads, and "every tap affects change." Prize a small magical core over more features. ([Sago Sago](https://kidscreen.com/2015/04/09/sago-sago-dishes-on-developing-for-the-preschool-market/), [Sago Mini](https://sagomini.com/article/learning-through-play-build-real-world-skills-with-sago-mini-world/), [Toca Boca](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/childs-play-free-to-play-is-a-no-no-when-it-comes-to-apps-for-kids-says-toca-bocas-emil-ovemar/))
- **Counters / streaks / variable-ratio rewards are a documented HARM for young children** — JAMA Network Open found these "dark patterns" common in apps used by 3–5yos; variable rewards mimic poker machines, streaks induce panic/shame. A precautionary stance against all of them is recommended. ([JAMA Netw Open](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2793493), [The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/the-design-tricks-keeping-your-kids-hooked-on-games-and-apps-and-3-things-you-can-do-about-it-239493), [Frontiers Digital Health](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fdgth.2022.822933/pdf))
- **Limit concurrent interactive elements** — kids-UX consensus is "one goal per screen," minimal elements, generous spacing; crowded screens confuse 2–4yos. ([Rovio](https://medium.com/@roviobert/ui-ux-guidelines-for-ages-3-6-68f1c9656574), [UX Collective](https://uxdesign.cc/designing-apps-for-young-kids-part-1-ff54c46c773b))

### Recommendation
The research endorses the decided design. Hold these specifics:
- **3 taps**, each landing its **own concrete crack stage on the egg** (never a progress bar). Three is a sensible ceiling — long enough to build anticipation, short enough to keep the loop tight. Ensure the burst always lands on the 3rd tap regardless of timing.
- **Forgive everything:** tappable target generously larger than the visible egg (aim ≥2cm physical), accept ~10mm offset and long presses, register on touch-down. A toddler mashing one egg always hatches it in 3 contacts.
- **Keep the wobble** as the anticipatory "tell"; pair every tap with simultaneous crack-sound + crack-visual, and a big fanfare on burst.
- **Multi-touch is correct here** (no wrong answer). Hold the **sandbox** line: no score/counter/streak/difficulty.
- **4 eggs** is the upper edge but defensible (identical, fixed, evenly spaced, self-contained targets); keep the rest of the screen quiet. Fall back to 3 if the ~2yo looks overwhelmed in playtest.
- Keep the **golden egg purely cosmetic / non-collectible** (no counter, can't be farmed) so it stays a delightful rare surprise, not an intermittent-reward hook.

### Open questions
- **Tap count for under-4s** has no hard source number; whether the ~2yo sustains intent across 3 deliberate taps is unverified — playtest, and consider a faster/auto-hatch for the youngest.
- **4 vs 3 concurrent eggs** sits in an evidence gap for identical fixed targets — only direct observation confirms it doesn't overwhelm.
- **Crack-stage legibility:** whether a 2–4yo reads three discrete crack stages as escalating "progress" (vs visual noise) on a wobbling object — stages may need to be exaggerated/distinct.

## 2. Architecture — structuring a multi-target tap-crack-reveal sandbox

### Key findings
- **A tiny explicit enum-FSM is the right shape for a small fixed set of modal stages.** Switch-on-state FSMs are "clean, easy to implement and maintain, especially for small FSMs"; class/state hierarchies and data-driven transition tables only pay off with reuse across actors or designer-reconfigurable transitions — neither applies to a 4-stage egg. ([Marymoor Studios FSM](https://marymoorstudios.com/devlog/2025-11-26-FSM), [slicker.me FSM](https://slicker.me/godot/finite-state-machines.html))
- **Use explicit states for modal behavior, variables for bounded accumulators.** Crack progression is a small accumulator (`taps` 0→3) mapping cleanly to a stage enum — no state explosion to avoid, so an enum (or just the tap count) is the correct model. ([Marymoor Studios FSM](https://marymoorstudios.com/devlog/2025-11-26-FSM))
- **"Black-box sim": commands in, events out, no engine/time knowledge.** The sim receives a command, updates state, emits events, and knows nothing of input devices, graphics, *or even time* — "all pacing, animation queuing and time management belong to the presentation layer." A tap is a command; crack/hatch/refill are events the scene animates. ([Black Box Sim](https://samuel-bouchet.fr/posts/2026-04-08-black-box-sim/))
- **Determinism contract for testable core:** a single seeded PRNG, `Math.random()` banned, `Date.now()`/`performance.now()` banned — "the simulation knows only its own tick counter." Matches the sibling-mode pattern (`pop.ts`, `gumballs.ts`). ([Subterrans seven principles](https://subterrans.com/devlog/the-seven-principles/), [hexagonal game logic](https://shendriks.dev/posts/2024-12-30-making-game-logic-framework-independent-with-hexagonal-architecture/))
- **`Phaser.Group` is the canonical pool;** `get()` reuses the first inactive member (allocates only when all are active), and **you must reset stale `active/visible/alpha/scale/angle/tint` on reuse** — a recycled object carries the prior occupant's dirty state. ([Ourcade pools](https://blog.ourcade.co/posts/2020/phaser-3-optimization-object-pool-basic/), [Phaser Group docs](https://docs.phaser.io/api-documentation/class/gameobjects-group))
- **The stale-tween pitfall is real:** always `tweens.killTweensOf(target)` before recycling/destroying; as of 3.60 tweens auto-destroy on completion unless `persist`, so a held reference can go stale. ([Phaser 3.60 TweenManager](https://github.com/photonstorm/phaser/blob/master/changelog/3.60/TweenManager.md), [2025 optimization writeup](https://franzeus.medium.com/how-i-optimized-my-phaser-3-action-game-in-2025-5a648753f62b))
- **Reuse one particle emitter, reposition + `explode()` per burst** — never construct an emitter per spawn (leaks). ([Phaser ParticleEmitter docs](https://docs.phaser.io/api-documentation/class/gameobjects-particles-particleemitter))
- **"Maintain population / max-alive"** = a loop that tops up to a cap when `alive < target` and decrements on despawn so the group refills later; pair with recycle-don't-allocate (relocate/reset in place). ([Scenario Spawner](https://github.com/PixelWizards/com.pixelwizards.scenariospawner), [pressure recycler](https://www.creativespore.com/docs/massive-swarm-system/spawners/pressure-recycler/))
- **Multi-touch needs `input.addPointer(n)`** (default gives only `mousePointer`+`pointer1`); a **single scene-level `pointerdown`** + your own nearest-target dispatch avoids the `setInteractive` traps — input order is registration order *not* depth, and only the top object fires unless `setTopOnly(false)`. ([Phaser InputPlugin docs](https://docs.phaser.io/api-documentation/class/input-inputplugin), [depth/order thread](https://phaser.discourse.group/t/tilemaplayer-input-and-depth/13822))
- **Decide contents at spawn, not at reveal** (the WoW loot precedent: "loot is determined the moment a creature spawns," concealed until the kill). This is exactly what lets the golden egg look special from birth. Tamper-proof commit-reveal only matters for networked/monetized reveals — not a local sandbox. ([WoW loot timing](https://blog.igv.com/wow-mythos-whoever-goes-into-the-dungeon-first-determines-the-loot/))

### Recommendation
**`src/core/eggs.ts` (pure, no Phaser, Vitest-tested, seeded RNG, no `Date.now()`):**
- Model the clutch as a fixed-size array of plain records: `{ id, slot, stage: 'intact'|'crack1'|'crack2'|'burst', contents, taps }`. `stage` is the explicit enum; `taps` the accumulator. Data, not classes.
- Pure functions, **commands in → events out**: `tapEgg(state, slot) → Event[]` advances that egg's stage and, on reaching `burst`, emits `Hatched` carrying the pre-decided `contents`; `refillSlot(state, slot) → Event[]` resets the slot to a fresh `intact` egg with newly-picked contents. A `maintainPopulation` helper caps at 4 and only refills empty slots.
- **Decide contents at spawn** via the **one shared** `createBag` picker (full 61-emoji set + gated jackpot) — a jackpot draw makes that egg spawn golden. Burst merely *reveals* the already-chosen contents (no second draw; fully deterministic under seed). One bag shared across all 4 slots keeps no-immediate-repeat and jackpot gating global.

**`EggsScene.ts` (thin render/adapter — owns all Phaser, timing, animation):**
- Two `Group` pools (egg sprites; revealed-animal sprites). On `get()`, reset `active/visible/alpha/scale/angle/tint`.
- **Tween hygiene:** `killTweensOf(sprite)` before recycling or re-tweening; the wobble is a looping tween — kill it on burst; the ~2–3s reveal fade does `killTweensOf` + `killAndHide` in `onComplete`, then asks core to `refillSlot` and spawns a fresh egg into the slot.
- **One shared burst emitter:** `setPosition(x,y)` + `explode(N)` per hatch; never re-create.
- **Input:** `addPointer(3)` + a single scene-level `pointerdown`; nearest-egg dispatch via the existing `pickNearestWithinRadius`. Keeps hit logic unit-testable and sidesteps the `setInteractive` gotchas.
- The scene translates core events to animations on its own clock (`time.delayedCall` for pacing); core never sees time.

### Open questions
- **Jackpot gating with 4 independent refills:** confirm the shared-bag draw-count gate behaves sensibly when 4 slots refill asynchronously (global gate is intended).
- **Taps during the ~2–3s reveal window:** keep `burst` as the terminal core stage and treat reveal/fade/refill as scene-only transient states (recommended), unless taps mid-reveal should do something — then a `revealing` core stage is warranted.
- **Multi-touch same-egg-same-frame:** decide whether two fingers resolving to one egg in a frame advance it twice or are debounced to one tap-per-egg-per-frame (recommend debounce in core).
- **Wobble phase** is cosmetic (scene-side); only push it through core if deterministic *visual* replay is ever wanted (it isn't, for a sandbox).

## 3. Tech Stack — Phaser 4 techniques & asset approach (stack is fixed)

### Key findings
- **Graphics draws crack lines** with `lineStyle` + `beginPath/moveTo/lineTo/strokePath`; `graphics.clear()` wipes so each stage can be redrawn from scratch — the natural way to do a 3-stage progressive crack on a shape-drawn egg. A jagged seam = midpoint-displacement (the 2D-lightning algorithm), neighbor displacement limited to avoid over-jaggedness. ([Phaser Graphics](https://docs.phaser.io/phaser/concepts/gameobjects/graphics), [2D lightning](https://code.tutsplus.com/how-to-generate-shockingly-good-2d-lightning-effects--gamedev-2681t))
- **Phaser 4 dropped Bitmap Masks; Geometry Masks are Canvas-only** (WebGL uses Mask Filters: `layer.filters.external.addMask`). A mask/erase "reveal" therefore costs more plumbing than just redrawing crack-line Graphics per stage. ([Masks in Phaser 4](https://phaser.discourse.group/t/masks-in-phaser-4/15268))
- **Shatter burst:** `emitter.explode([count], x, y)` "stops any current flow and emits several particles at once," aimable at an arbitrary point — one persistent emitter, repositioned and re-`explode()`'d per hatch. Shard look = `tint`, `gravityY`, `speed`/`angle` range, `scale`/`alpha` ramps. ([ParticleEmitter 4.0](https://docs.phaser.io/api-documentation/4.0.0/class/gameobjects-particles-particleemitter), [phaser3-faq Particles](https://github.com/samme/phaser3-faq/wiki/Particles))
- **Wobble** = a rotation tween `{ angle: ±4, ease: 'Sine.easeInOut', yoyo: true, repeat: -1, duration ~500–700 }` (in-out eases "work well with yo-yos"). ([Phaser 4 tween skill](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/phaser@4.2.0/skills/tweens/SKILL.md), [phaser3-faq Tweens](https://github.com/samme/phaser3-faq/wiki/Tweens))
- **Golden shimmer:** Phaser 4 has a first-class **Shine** FX (`gameObject.postFX.addShine(speed, lineWidth, gradient)`) passing a specular band across the object, Tween-paced so it stays slow/non-strobing; a slow gold tint pulse (`setTintMode(ADD)`) is a cheap alternative. ([Phaser 4 FX](https://docs.phaser.io/phaser/concepts/fx), [v4 RC notes](https://phaser.io/news/2026/03/phaser-v4-release-candidate-7))
- **An egg outline has no exact equation;** clean shape-drawn options: the asymmetry curve `x²/a² + y²/b²·(1+kx) = 1` (small `k` → pointed top; `k=0` is an ellipse) sampled to a polygon, or two/three joined circular arcs of different radii (`arc()` calls). ([egg equation](https://www.johndcook.com/blog/2018/04/18/equation-to-fit-an-egg/), [egg from arcs](https://cscheng.info/2016/06/05/drawing-egg-shapes-with-p5js.html))
- **"Tok/crack" SFX = filtered-noise percussion:** a tiny (~8ms) exponential-decay white-noise buffer through a **bandpass** biquad (~3–5kHz, Q≈3); raise the center frequency per tap for the 3 rising "toks." MDN's canonical noise recipe is the same. ([Web Audio UI sfx](https://www.mintlify.com/raphaelsalaja/userinterface-wiki/technical/web-audio-api), [MDN advanced techniques](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Web_Audio_API/Advanced_techniques))
- **Shatter sound** = a longer (~150–200ms) bandpassed noise burst, `gain` 0.9→0.001 exponential decay, optionally a fast downward pitch transient for body (the standard kick/snare-click recipe). ([kick drum Web Audio](https://webaudio.studio/blog/how-to-make-a-kick-drum-with-the-web-audio-api/))
- **Photosensitivity:** keep flashes ≤ 3/sec (Section 508 bars 3–55Hz flicker), with saturated **red** held to a stricter limit — so the gold shimmer stays sub-1Hz/slow and avoids red strobes; the reduced-motion branch reads `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` via `matchMedia`. ([WCAG three-flashes](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/three-flashes-or-below-threshold), [MDN seizure disorders](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/Accessibility/Guides/Seizure_disorders), [prefers-reduced-motion](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/At-rules/%40media/prefers-reduced-motion))
- **Asset note:** Noto's animated set *does* include 🐣 Hatching Chick (1f423, v15.0), addressable via the [animation API](https://googlefonts.github.io/noto-emoji-animation/), but these are standalone loops, not a coordinated crack→hatch sequence — and a Noto egg can't be progressively cracked. ([Emojipedia animated Noto 15.0](https://emojipedia.org/animated-noto-color-emoji/15.0/hatching-chick))

### Recommendation
- **Draw the egg with Graphics, not emoji** (egg-curve equation or stacked arcs). It must visibly crack/shatter, and Phaser 4's mask changes make "erase the shell" awkward. Reserve the existing animated-emoji set only for the **animal that pops out**.
- **Progressive crack = redraw, not mask:** one Graphics overlay; on each of the 3 taps `clear()` and stroke the accumulated cracks (stage 1: one short jagged seam; stage 2: add a branch; stage 3: full web), seams generated by the jaggedness-limited midpoint-displacement routine.
- **Shatter = one pooled emitter, `explode(N, x, y)`** with a tiny shell-shard texture (Graphics → `generateTexture`), `gravityY` so shards fall, tint = eggshell (gold for golden eggs).
- **Wobble + shimmer:** ±4° `Sine.easeInOut` yoyo `repeat:-1`; golden shimmer via `postFX.addShine()` slow + optional gold ADD-tint pulse — both well under 3 flashes/sec, no saturated red.
- **Barnyard background, Graphics-drawn:** `fillGradientStyle` sky, a grass band, and a straw nest from layered radiating thin gold/brown strokes — consistent with the no-image-asset convention.
- **SFX synthesized offline** in a node build script (mirrors `build-peekaboo-audio.mjs`): 3 "tok" cracks (rising bandpass centers ~3.5/4.2/5kHz, Q≈3) + 1 "shatter" (~150–200ms bandpassed noise, exp decay), encoded to MP3 with the present `@breezystack/lamejs`. Reuse `Celebrations`/`Sound.fanfare()`/`tada()` for the animal-pop flourish + golden party.
- **Reduced-motion variant:** on `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, drop wobble/shimmer and replace the shard explosion with a quick shell fade/scale.

### Open questions
- Right **shard-texture source** (pre-baked `generateTexture` quad vs drawn triangle) and **shard count N** that reads as "shatter" without hurting mobile perf.
- Whether a Phaser 4 **Mask Filter** shell-erase is ever nicer than redraw-Graphics for the *final* shatter frame — a small spike could tell.
- Exact **gold-shimmer cadence** (Shine `speed`/tween duration) that reads "shiny" while staying inside the 3-flash/saturated-red limits — tune by eye.
- Whether to use the Noto **Hatching Chick (1f423)** as a thematic pop-out for some eggs, or keep the pop-out drawn purely from the existing 61-creature set.

## Summary → into the spec
The intake survives research intact. Build: a pure `src/core/eggs.ts` (enum-FSM clutch + shared `createBag` picker, contents-at-spawn, seeded, Vitest-tested) driving a thin `EggsScene` (4 wobble spots, redraw-crack Graphics egg, one pooled shatter emitter, multi-touch nearest-dispatch, reveal→fade→refill loop), a Graphics **barnyard** `EggsBackground`, offline-synthesized crack/shatter SFX, a swappable music track, and a +1 title button. Hold the sandbox line; keep the golden egg cosmetic; 3 forgiving taps with concrete per-tap crack stages; photosensitivity-safe + reduced-motion by construction.
